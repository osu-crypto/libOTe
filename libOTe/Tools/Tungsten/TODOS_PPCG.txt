NOTES

n choose w distribution (initial)
- not a normal distribution (primarily, it is discrete, not continuous), much more similar to binomial distribution
-not a probability distribution since (1) k choose w are counts, not probabilities, (2) the sum is non-normalized
- the sum in probability distributions needs to be 1
- we know that sum from w=0 to w=n of (n choose w), for a fixed n, is 2^n 
- clearly 2^n != 1
- n choose w represents the number of ways to choose w successes out of n trials
- binomial distribution  shows the likelihood of each possible number of successes (w) in n independent Bernoulli trials, 
given the probability of success p for each trial.
- Observe, just like binomial distribution for p=0.5, the peak is at n/2, the curve is symmetric, and discrete
- can we convert n choose w distribution to binomial distribution?
- To convert the n choose w values into a valid probability distribution, you can normalize them 
by dividing each value by the total sum 2^n, i.e. (n choose w) / 2^n for all w
- then we get binomial distribution parameterized by n and p=0.5
- we now know the mean is at np = 0.5n and the variance is np(1-p) = 0.5n(1-0.5) = 0.25n
- also note that for larger n, the curve approximates gaussian distribution due to central limit theorem
- the mean stays same for following iterations, but the variance shrinks
- maybe we can use it to compute the distribution in the next iterations of permute-multiply (while dealing with much smaller numbers)

Distribution after repeater (expansion factor 2) and a single iteration of permute-multiply

 - as we run permute-multiply iterations, the mean of the distributions stays the same -> n/2
 - it is still bell curved with rapid increase/decrease (as in n choose w distributions)
 - the difference is that the distribution gets narrower (symmetrically on both left and right side), 
 i.e. it becomes steeper and narrower
 -after expansion with a repeater (assuming expansion is 2), every other number on the distribution is 0, otherwise
 the curve is similar to n choose w
 - after the permute-multiply, it is similar to Binomial Distribution with Large n, could be approximated with gaussian distribution
